cityfolks club of nigeria

Cityfolks Club of Nigeria is a dynamic organization with a multifaceted approach to achieving its goals. By prioritizing friendship, support, community outreach, education, fundraising, and effective public relations, the club strives to make a meaningful impact on the lives of its members and the broader society.

The organization is committed to moral and financial assistance for its members, outreach to the needy in the community, educational support through scholarships, fundraising initiatives, and establishing strong relationships with the public.

club history

The Cityfolks Club of Nigeria is a vibrant organization that was established by a group of dedicated professionals in Abeokuta City. The primary objective of the club is to serve humanity, fostering friendship, and providing a platform for socializing and networking. It is important to note that the club is entirely apolitical, with a firm interest in supporting national development initiatives. The pioneer President, Folk Adesina Sodiya, officially initiated the Cityfolks Club, providing leadership from 1996 to 1997, and the symbol of membership for the Cityfolks Club is the term "Folk." Since its inception, the club has seen various individuals serve as President, and in its commitment to continuity and stability, the tenure of the President has been upgraded to a minimum of two years, or more terms if elected. In 2008, the Cityfolks Club was formally incorporated, receiving registration number 29877 and establishing its address at Sodimu House, opposite White House, Labayiwa Oke Aregba, Abeokuta, Ogun State. Following Folk Adesina Sodiya's presidency, a number of esteemed individuals, namely Folk Femi Yusuf, Folk Segun, Folk Ayodeji Adeleye, Folk Tunde Atobatele, Folk Biodun Badmus, Folk Afolabi Bakare, Folk Biodun Coker, Folk Chris Boniwe, Folk Afolabi Adekanmbi, Folk Niyi Adeleke, Folk Sina Sanusi, and the outgoing President, Folk Taiwo Ajayi, have assumed the role of President. Currently, the City Folks Club is preparing to inaugurate its 13th President, Folk Lanre Jaji. The club's guiding motto, "Many Folks... One Fold," epitomizes its dedication to unity, collaboration, and the collective well-being of its members and the broader community. In recognition of notable contributions, the club has had esteemed honorary members such as Alhaji Badru, the former AGM of Ogun State Water Corporation, Otunba Debo Okunowo, the head of the Lagos office of OGBC, and Chief Yemi Sanusi, who championed the Cityfolks project. The club is grateful for their moral and financial support. The Cityfolks Club's historical heroes, including Arch Bola Ogunbayo and the late Aroyeun, have played pivotal roles in shaping the organization. Additionally, individuals such as Tunji Soremi, Tunde Atobatele, Segun Adeleye, and Sina Sodiya were instrumental in ensuring the club's establishment despite the challenges faced during its formative stage.

Club activities

CityFolks club of Nigeria is committed to fostering a sense of community through the maintenance of a dedicated Club House. The Social Secretary plays a pivotal role in ensuring the smooth day-to-day functioning of the facility, with regular reporting on activities and financial matters. Our unique meeting setup at the Club House encourages members to bring their own meals, creating a relaxed and enjoyable atmosphere for all. The club held its inaugural meeting in October 1995 at 12 Imo Street, Abeokuta, with four original members, eventually expanding to 12 graduates. Noteworthy figures such as Mrs. M.B.T. Obe, the Treasury Manager of Gateway Bank of Nigeria Plc, and Mr. Ayo Ogunbanjo, the Managing Partner of Ayo Ogunbanjo and Co. Estate Surveyors and Valuers, have been pivotal to the club's early development. As the Cityfolks Club looks forward to inaugurating its 13th President, the spirit of unity and collaboration encapsulated in its motto, "Many Folks... One Fold," continues to guide the organization towards a future of growth and prosperity.
